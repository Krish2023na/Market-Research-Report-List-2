<p><p>Advanced CO2 sensors are used to monitor and measure carbon dioxide levels in different environments. NDIR (non-dispersive infrared) CO2 sensors work by emitting infrared light and measuring the amount of light absorbed by CO2 molecules, providing accurate readings. Chemical CO2 sensors use chemical reactions to detect CO2 levels, often using a pH indicator that changes color based on the presence of CO2. Both types of sensors are commonly used in industries, buildings, and vehicles to monitor and control CO2 levels for energy management, air quality, and safety purposes.</p></p>
