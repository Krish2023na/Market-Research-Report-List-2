<p><p>The Next Generation Sequencing (NGS) market encompasses three main types. Targeted sequencing focuses on specific regions of the genome to identify variations associated with certain diseases. Resequencing involves comparing an individual's genome to a reference genome to find differences. Whole genome sequencing analyzes an individual's entire DNA to generate a comprehensive view of their genetic makeup. Lastly, whole exome sequencing examines only the protein-coding regions of the genome, which are often associated with disease-causing variations, making it more cost-effective than whole genome sequencing.</p></p>
