<p><p>A podoscope is a medical device used in podiatry to examine the foot's pressure distribution. It consists of a glass plate with built-in lighting, a mirror, and a camera that captures images of the patient's feet when standing on the glass plate. The images help podiatrists analyze the weight distribution and identify potential foot abnormalities or conditions. Podoscopes are widely used in clinics, hospitals, and orthopedic centers.</p><p>The global podoscope market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue this trend. The market is primarily driven by the rising prevalence of foot-related disorders and the increasing awareness regarding foot care. As foot problems such as plantar fasciitis, diabetic foot ulcers, and flat feet become more common, the demand for podoscopy devices and services grows.</p><p>Additionally, technological advancements in podoscope design, such as the integration of digital imaging and computer-aided analysis software, have improved the accuracy and efficiency of foot examinations.
