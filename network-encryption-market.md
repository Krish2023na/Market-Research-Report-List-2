<p><ul><li>Digital Encryption</li><li>Symmetric and Asymmetric Encryption</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Network encryption is a vital tool for securing communication networks. The market encompasses three main types: digital encryption, symmetric encryption, and asymmetric encryption. Digital encryption refers to the conversion of data into a code form, making it unreadable without proper decoding keys. Symmetric encryption employs a shared key by both the sender and recipient to encrypt and decrypt data, making it faster but less secure compared to asymmetric encryption. Asymmetric encryption involves the use of two keys, public and private, ensuring greater security as data encrypted with one key can only be decrypted with the other key.</p></p>
