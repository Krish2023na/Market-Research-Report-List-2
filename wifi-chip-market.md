<p><p>The WiFi chip market can be categorized into various types based on the generations of WiFi technology they support. WiFi-4 chips, also known as 802.11n, were prevalent in the early 2000s. WiFi-5 chips, or 802.11ac, came next and offered faster speeds and improved performance. WiFi-6 chips, or 802.11ax, introduced in 2019, brought significant advancements in speed, capacity, and efficiency. WiFi-6E chips further expand WiFi-6 capabilities by operating in the newly opened 6 GHz frequency band, allowing for additional spectrum and reduced congestion.</p></p>
