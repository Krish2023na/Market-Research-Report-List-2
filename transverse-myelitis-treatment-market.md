<p><p>Transverse myelitis is a rare neurological disorder characterized by inflammation of the spinal cord that results in various neurological symptoms such as weakness, numbness, and problems with bladder and bowel control. The treatment for transverse myelitis aims to reduce inflammation, manage symptoms, and prevent complications.</p><p>The primary treatment for transverse myelitis includes high-dose intravenous corticosteroids to reduce inflammation in the spinal cord. In severe cases, plasma exchange therapy or intravenous immunoglobulins may be used. Physical therapy, occupational therapy, and supportive care are also important in managing the symptoms and improving quality of life for patients.</p><p>The transverse myelitis treatment market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
