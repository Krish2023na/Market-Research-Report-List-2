<p><p>Insertion electromagnetic flow meters are devices that are used to measure the flow rate of conductive liquids in pipes. They work on the principle of Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction, which states that when a conductor moves through a magnetic field, a voltage is induced across the conductor. Insertion electromagnetic flow meters consist of a sensor that is inserted into the pipe, and a transmitter that converts the voltage generated by the sensor into a flow rate measurement.</p><p>The insertion electromagnetic flow meters market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.9% during the forecast period.
