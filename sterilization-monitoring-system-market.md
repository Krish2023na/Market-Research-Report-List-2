<p><p>The sterilization monitoring system market includes three types of monitoring systems: biological, chemical, and mechanical. Biological monitoring systems use spore strips or vials to verify the efficiency of sterilization processes by measuring the viability of microorganisms. Chemical monitoring systems involve the use of indicators or test strips that change color when certain parameters, such as temperature or exposure to sterilizing agents, are met. Mechanical monitoring systems assess the physical aspects of sterilization equipment, such as time, temperature, and pressure, to ensure proper functioning and accurate results.</p></p>
