<p><ul><li>Intraoral X-Rays</li><li>Extraoral X-Rays</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dental X-rays are an essential part of oral healthcare. There are two main types of dental X-rays: intraoral and extraoral. Intraoral X-rays are taken inside the mouth and provide detailed images of individual teeth, roots, and surrounding tissues, helping dentists diagnose cavities, gum disease, and other dental issues. Extraoral X-rays are taken outside the mouth and capture a broader view of the skull and jaw, assisting in the assessment of impacted teeth, jaw development, and detecting tumors or fractures. Both types of X-rays contribute to accurate dental diagnoses and treatment planning.</p></p>
