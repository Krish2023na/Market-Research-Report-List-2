<p><p>The overhead cables market encompasses two main categories: low voltage cables and high voltage cables. Low voltage cables are designed for electrical systems that carry a lower amount of voltage, typically used for residential and small-scale applications. High voltage cables, on the other hand, are engineered to handle significantly higher voltage levels, primarily used in utility power transmission and distribution networks. Both types of cables are installed above ground, typically on poles or towers, and play a crucial role in delivering electricity to homes, businesses, and industries.</p></p>
