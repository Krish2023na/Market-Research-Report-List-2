One of the key factors driving this growth is the increasing prevalence of sleep apnea and the subsequent rise in the number of CPAP users. This has led to a surge in demand for CPAP devices, thus driving the need for cleaners and sanitizers.</p><p>Additionally, the awareness about the importance of good hygiene in maintaining the effectiveness of CPAP equipment has also increased. Proper cleaning and sanitizing of CPAP devices help prevent the buildup of bacteria, mold, and other pathogens, reducing the risk of respiratory infections. As a result, individuals are increasingly investing in CPAP cleaners and sanitizers to ensure the longevity and safe use of their equipment.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements have led to the development of portable and automated CPAP cleaners and sanitizers, offering convenience and ease of use.
