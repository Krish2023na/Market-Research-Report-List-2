<p><p>The heat-resistant inorganic adhesive market encompasses various types such as silicate, sulfate, phosphate, borate, and others. Silicate-based adhesives are known for their excellent adhesion and resistance to heat, making them suitable for applications in high-temperature environments. Sulfate-based adhesives offer good heat resistance along with high chemical resistance. Phosphate-based adhesives provide both heat and fire resistance, making them ideal for fireproofing applications. Borate-based adhesives exhibit excellent thermal stability and are widely used in the construction industry. Other types of heat-resistant inorganic adhesives include those derived from metals and metal oxides, offering unique properties for specific applications.</p></p>
