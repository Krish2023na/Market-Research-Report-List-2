<p><ul><li>SD-WAN</li><li>Traditional WAN</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Enterprise WAN market encompasses two primary types: SD-WAN and Traditional WAN. SD-WAN, or Software-Defined WAN, is a modern approach that utilizes software-defined networking technology to dynamically optimize traffic routing across multiple connections, potentially including MPLS, broadband internet, or cellular networks. This enhances network performance, increases flexibility, and reduces costs. In contrast, Traditional WAN employs traditional hardware-based networking solutions, such as dedicated leased lines or MPLS circuits, providing reliable but limited scalability and flexibility. SD-WAN offers a more agile and cost-effective alternative to the conventional and rigid nature of Traditional WAN.</p></p>
