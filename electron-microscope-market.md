<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Electron microscopes have two main types: scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and transmission electron microscopy (TEM). SEM scans the surface of a sample with a focused beam of electrons to create a detailed 3D image. It is widely used in materials science, nanotechnology, and biology research. On the other hand, TEM uses a beam of electrons to pass through a sample, capturing its internal structure. It is vital for studying ultra-thin samples with high resolution, making it valuable for biological and material research at the atomic level.</p></p>
