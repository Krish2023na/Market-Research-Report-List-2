<p><p>Nucleic Acid Testing (NAT) is a market that includes various techniques for the detection and analysis of nucleic acids. Different types of NAT techniques are used, such as Transcription-mediated Amplification (TMA), Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR), Ligase Chain Reaction (LCR), and Whole Genome Sequencing. TMA amplifies RNA to detect and quantify viral infections. PCR replicates and amplifies DNA to identify and analyze specific genetic sequences. LCR uses ligases to amplify specific DNA sequences. Whole Genome Sequencing determines an organism's complete DNA sequence, allowing for comprehensive genetic analysis.</p></p>
