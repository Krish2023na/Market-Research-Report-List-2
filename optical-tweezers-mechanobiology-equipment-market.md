<p><p>Optical tweezers and magnetic tweezers are two types of mechanobiology equipment used in scientific research. Optical tweezers utilize focused laser beams to trap and manipulate microscopic particles, such as cells or molecules, in a non-contact manner. They can measure and control mechanical forces at the nano and pico Newton scale. On the other hand, magnetic tweezers employ magnetic fields to manipulate magnetic particles or nanobeads that are attached to the target biological sample, enabling the study of mechanical properties and forces at the molecular level.</p></p>
