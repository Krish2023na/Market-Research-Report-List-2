<p><p>Microwave diodes are electronic devices used in microwave systems for signal handling and control. The market for microwave diodes consists of various types such as Schottky diodes, Gunn diodes, tunnel diodes, Zener diodes, and PIN diodes. Schottky diodes utilize a metal-semiconductor junction, while Gunn diodes generate microwave signals by exploiting the Gunn effect in certain semiconductors. Tunnel diodes utilize quantum tunneling, Zener diodes work in reverse breakdown, and PIN diodes are formed with a p-doped intrinsic semiconductor sandwiched between n-doped and p-doped regions, used for various applications in microwave circuitry.</p></p>
