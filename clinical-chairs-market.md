<p><p>Clinical chairs are medical devices used in healthcare facilities for examining and treating patients. There are three types of clinical chairs: manual, semi-automated, and fully-automated. Manual clinical chairs require manual adjustments for height, backrest, and legrest positions. Semi-automated clinical chairs have some automated features for height adjustment, but still require manual adjustments for other positions. Fully-automated clinical chairs are equipped with motors that can adjust the height, backrest, legrest, and other positions with the push of a button. These different market types provide healthcare professionals with varying levels of convenience and efficiency in patient care.</p></p>
