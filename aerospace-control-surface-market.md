<p><p>The aerospace control surface market consists of various types of control surfaces used in aircraft for improved aerodynamic control and stability. Flaps are movable surfaces on the wings that increase lift during takeoff and landing. Slats are movable panels on the leading edge of the wings that enhance lift at low speeds. Spoilers are deployed to reduce lift and increase drag. Ailerons are found on the wings and provide roll control. Elevators on the tail section control the pitch of the aircraft. Lastly, the rudder located on the tail controls the yaw motion.</p></p>
