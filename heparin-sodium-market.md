<p><p>Heparin sodium, also known as unfractionated heparin (UFH), is an anticoagulant medication used to prevent and treat blood clots. It works by enhancing the activity of antithrombin III, a natural protein in the body that inhibits blood clotting. Heparin sodium is commonly used in medical settings such as hospitals and clinics, as well as during surgeries and dialysis procedures.</p><p>The global heparin sodium market is projected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to several factors, including the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, rising demand for anticoagulant therapies, and growth in the geriatric population. Additionally, the market is driven by the growing awareness among healthcare professionals regarding the benefits of heparin sodium, as well as the development of technologically advanced heparin sodium formulations.</p><p>One of the latest trends observed in the heparin sodium market is the shift towards low molecular weight heparins (LMWHs). These LMWHs offer advantages over UFH, including greater bioavailability, prolonged half-life, decreased risk of bleeding, and predictable anticoagulation effects. This trend is expected to drive the demand for LMWHs and impact the growth of the heparin sodium market.</p><p>Furthermore, increasing research and development activities aiming to enhance the therapeutic efficacy and safety profile of heparin sodium are also expected to contribute to market growth. Manufacturers are focusing on developing novel formulations and delivery mechanisms, such as oral heparin, to improve patient compliance and convenience.</p><p>In conclusion, the heparin sodium market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12.3% during the forecast period.
