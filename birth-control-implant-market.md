<p><p>A birth control implant is a small, flexible rod that is inserted under the skin of a woman's upper arm to prevent pregnancy. It releases a hormone called progestin, which works by stopping the release of eggs from the ovaries, thickening the cervical mucus to prevent sperm from reaching the egg, and thinning the lining of the uterus to prevent the implantation of a fertilized egg. This form of contraceptive is highly effective, with a success rate of more than 99%.</p><p>The birth control implant market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue its upward trajectory during the forecast period. The increasing awareness about family planning and the need for effective contraception methods are the primary factors driving market growth. Additionally, the rising prevalence of unwanted pregnancies and the growing demand for long-acting reversible contraceptives (LARCs) further contribute to the market expansion.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in implant technologies, such as the development of smaller and more durable implants, are also supporting market growth.
