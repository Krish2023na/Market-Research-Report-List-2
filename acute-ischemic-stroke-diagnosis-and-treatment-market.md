<p><p>Acute ischemic stroke diagnosis and treatment refer to the medical procedures and therapies used to diagnose and manage patients with this type of stroke, which occurs when a blood clot blocks an artery that supplies blood to the brain. The goal is to quickly restore blood flow to the affected area of the brain to minimize damage and improve patient outcomes.</p><p>Diagnosis of acute ischemic stroke typically involves a physical examination, imaging tests such as computed tomography (CT) scans or magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and blood tests. Treatment options include the administration of clot-busting medications such as tissue plasminogen activator (tPA), mechanical thrombectomy (surgical removal of the clot), and supportive care to manage symptoms and prevent complications.</p><p>The market for acute ischemic stroke diagnosis and treatment is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
