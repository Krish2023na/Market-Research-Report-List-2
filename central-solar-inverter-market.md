<p><p>Central solar inverters are used to convert the direct current (DC) generated by solar panels into alternating current (AC) that can be utilized by the power grid. Power Frequency Inverters operate at a utility frequency of 50 or 60 Hz, making them suitable for large-scale installations. Medium Frequency Inverters, operating between 3 to 30 kHz, offer improved efficiency and reduced size compared to power frequency inverters. High Frequency Inverters operate at frequencies above 30 kHz, providing even higher efficiency and smaller form factors, making them ideal for residential and commercial installations.</p></p>
