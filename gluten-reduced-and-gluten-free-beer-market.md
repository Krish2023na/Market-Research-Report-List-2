<p><p>Gluten-reduced and gluten-free beers are specially crafted beverages that cater to individuals with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease. Gluten is a protein found in grains like wheat, barley, and rye, which can cause adverse reactions in such individuals. Gluten-reduced beers are made using traditional ingredients but undergo a process to remove or reduce the gluten content. On the other hand, gluten-free beers are made using alternative grains like sorghum, millet, rice, or corn, which naturally do not contain gluten.</p><p>The gluten-reduced and gluten-free beer market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, primarily driven by the increasing prevalence of gluten-related disorders and the rising demand for gluten-free products. Craft breweries have started focusing on producing gluten-free or gluten-reduced variants, expanding the range of options available to consumers. Additionally, collaboration between breweries and health organizations has led to the development of innovative techniques to reduce or eliminate gluten from beers without compromising on taste or quality.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a growing trend of organic and premium gluten-free beers, targeting a niche segment of consumers seeking high-quality products. <p><p>The gluten-free beer market consists of two main types: dedicated gluten-free beers and gluten-reduced beers. Dedicated gluten-free beers are brewed using gluten-free ingredients such as sorghum, rice, or millet, ensuring that they do not contain any gluten throughout the brewing process. On the other hand, gluten-reduced beers are made using traditional brewing methods with gluten-containing grains like barley, but enzymes are added during fermentation to break down the gluten proteins. However, it is important to note that gluten-reduced beers may still contain trace amounts of gluten.</p></p>
