<p><p>The veterinary vaccine adjuvants market is divided into three types: oral, subcutaneous, and intramuscular. Oral vaccines are administered through the animal's mouth and swallowed, allowing for easy absorption in the gastrointestinal tract. Subcutaneous vaccines are injected just beneath the skin, stimulating the immune response at the injection site. Intramuscular vaccines are injected into the muscle tissue, which enhances the immune response due to the rich supply of immune cells in this area. These different administration routes provide options for effective delivery and immune stimulation in animals.</p></p>
