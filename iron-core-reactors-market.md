<p><p>Iron core reactors are widely used in electrical power systems to regulate and stabilize voltage. Two common types of iron core reactors are Dry Type and Oil Immersed Type. Dry Type reactors use air as the cooling medium and do not require any external cooling system. They are compact, lightweight, and suitable for low to medium voltage applications. On the other hand, Oil Immersed Type reactors are submerged in oil for cooling purposes. They are mainly used in high-power applications and provide better cooling efficiency and improved performance at higher voltages.</p></p>
