<p><ul><li>Beta Titanium</li><li>Nickel Titanium</li><li>Stainless Steel</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Orthodontic archwire is classified into various types based on the material used for its construction. Beta Titanium archwires possess excellent flexibility and shape memory, making them suitable for initial tooth alignment. Nickel Titanium archwires exhibit superelastic properties, allowing them to deliver a constant force throughout the treatment. Stainless Steel archwires provide high stiffness and are commonly used for finishing treatment. The "Other" market comprises archwires made from different materials, including polymers, cobalt-chromium alloys, and composite materials, which offer unique properties and applications in specific orthodontic cases.</p></p>
