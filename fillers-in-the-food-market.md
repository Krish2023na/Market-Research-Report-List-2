<p><p>Fillers are substances added to food to increase bulk, improve texture, and reduce costs. Starch, a common filler, is derived from plants like corn or potatoes. It enhances thickness and stability in sauces, soups, and baked goods. Cellulose is derived from plant cell walls and acts as a bulking agent in low-calorie foods. Calcium carbonate, derived from limestone, is a filler used in baking and as a dietary supplement. Talc, a mineral filler, improves texture in powdered products. Other fillers may include powdered milk, corn syrup solids, and maltodextrin, among others.</p></p>
